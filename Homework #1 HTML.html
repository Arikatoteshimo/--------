January 14, 1997

HTML 3.2 was published as W3C Recommendation. it was the first version developed and
standardized exclusively by the W3C as the IETF had closed its HTML Working Group on September
12, 1996.Initially code-named Wilbur, HTML 3.2 dropped math formulas entirely, reconciled overlap
among various proprietary extensions and adopted most of Netscape's visual markup tags. Netscape's
blink element and Microsoft's marquee element were omitted due to a mutual agreement between the
two companies.

A markup for mathematical formulas simalar to that in HTML was not standardized until 14 months laten in
MathML.
